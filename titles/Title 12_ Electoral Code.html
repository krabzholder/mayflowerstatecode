12 M.S.C. 2 § 6103VOTING EMERGENCY. Because of the existing and continuing possibility of an emergency or common disaster occurring before or during a regularly scheduled or special election, and in order to ensure maximum citizen participation in the electoral process and provide a safe and orderly procedure for persons seeking to exercise their right to vote, generally to minimize to whatever degree possible a person's exposure to danger during declared states of emergency, and to protect the integrity of the electoral process, it is hereby found and declared to be necessary to designate a procedure for the emergency suspension or delay and rescheduling of elections. The Governor or executive authority of any jurisdiction, upon consultation with the Chief Elections Officer, shall reschedule any election suspended or delayed due to an emergency. 12 M.S.C. 2 § 6304CHALLENGE TO BALLOT. When the right to vote of any person who desires to vote is questioned by any auditor or by the Chief Elections Officer or their staff, the challenge shall be reduced to writing with an oath of authenticity, giving reasons for the challenge, which shall be delivered to the Chief Elections Officer. A ballot may be challenged, even if the person holds residency, on the basis that it is an alternate account or was falsified. If there exists evidence and cause to believe such, the ballot shall be rejected.N/AACTIVE
