12 M.S.C. 2 § 3201RESIDENCY. All signatures on any given petition must be by a qualified electors of Clark County. Any signature done by someone that cannot be verified to have been affixed by someone with residency in Clark County shall be rejected.N/AACTIVE
106
12 M.S.C. 2 § 3202ENGLISH REQUIREMENT. All signatures must be made in the English language with clear and comprehensible words. Any signature that cannot reasonably be deciphered shall be rejected.N/AACTIVE
107
12 M.S.C. 2 § 3203FORCE, FEAR, OR INTIMIDATION. No signature shall be collected with the employment of force, fear, or intimidation of any qualified elector. If at any point, a qualified elector demonstrates or can testify that they were the victim of these practices in exchange for their signature, the petition shall be rejected in whole.N/AACTIVE
108
12 M.S.C. 2 § 3204BRIBERY. No signature shall be valid if made in exchange for money compensation, gift, or other form of quid pro quo. If at any point, it can be demonstrated that a signature was collected through bribery, the petition shall be rejected in whole.N/AACTIVE
109
12 M.S.C. 2 § 3205CANDIDATE TRANSPARENCY. For a signature to be validated, the candidate must present their username and the specific election that they are running for; or, alternatively, the candidate should show the petition to the elector — but above all, it is necessary that the elector be informed of the candidate and the race they are running for.N/AACTIVE
110
12 M.S.C. 2 § 3206PROOF OF SIGNATURE. All signatures must contain proof that the signature was made by the person that the signature reflects. The form of evidence may include photography, screenshot, etc. If there exists any doubt to the validity of a signature on a given petition, the signatory shall be contacted and asked if they signed their name on the petition, as well as if they knew what the purpose of the petition is. If there exists any doubt that the signature is invalid, fraudulent, or otherwise consistent with these rules or by law, the signature shall be rejected and the remainder of the petition shall be scrutinized in a similar fashion.N/AACTIVE
111
12 M.S.C. 2 § 3207DEMONSTRABLE FRAUD. If at any point during the verification of a petition or a signature, it can be demonstrated that the prospective candidate knowingly engaged in fraud in an intent to defraud the Secretary of State’s Election Division or to alter election processes, the petition shall be rejected in whole and the findings be referred for prosecution, as well as possible blacklist from all future elections as allowed by law.N/AACTIVE
112
12 M.S.C. 2 § 3208AFFIDAVIT. Once a petition has been submitted to the Chief Election’s Officer for review, it shall be treated as a signed affidavit affirming that all signatures on the petition are true and lawful to the knowledge of the prospective candidate signing.N/AACTIVE
113
12 M.S.C. 2 § 3209ALTERATION OF PETITION. It shall be against these rules to alter the title or purpose of any petition once it has been signed by a qualified elector. Any signature collected after alteration shall be invalidated. Further, the alteration of a petition after submission with the Chief Election Officer shall result in the petition being rejected in whole.N/AACTIVE

12 M.S.C. 2 § 6103VOTING EMERGENCY. Because of the existing and continuing possibility of an emergency or common disaster occurring before or during a regularly scheduled or special election, and in order to ensure maximum citizen participation in the electoral process and provide a safe and orderly procedure for persons seeking to exercise their right to vote, generally to minimize to whatever degree possible a person's exposure to danger during declared states of emergency, and to protect the integrity of the electoral process, it is hereby found and declared to be necessary to designate a procedure for the emergency suspension or delay and rescheduling of elections. The Governor or executive authority of any jurisdiction, upon consultation with the Chief Elections Officer, shall reschedule any election suspended or delayed due to an emergency. The election shall be held within ten (10) days after the date of the suspended or delayed election or as soon thereafter as is practicable.N/AACTIVE
